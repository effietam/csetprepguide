What Subtest II Tests

Three Content Domains

Each domain carries roughly equal weight. No domain can be skipped.

📖

Domain A — Spanish Literature

Medieval (Cantar de Mio Cid, La Celestina) · Renaissance (Garcilaso, picaresque) · Golden Age (Cervantes, Lope, Calderón, Góngora, Quevedo) · Romanticism (Bécquer) · Realism and Naturalism (Galdós, Clarín) · Generation of '98 (Unamuno, Machado) · Generation of '27 (Lorca) · Postwar fiction (Cela, Laforet)

~33%
🌎

Domain B — Latin American Literature

Colonial (Sor Juana, Las Casas) · Independence era (Martí) · Modernismo (Darío) · Criollismo, Negrismo (Guillén) · Boom (García Márquez, Vargas Llosa, Cortázar, Fuentes) · Magical realism and its precursors (Carpentier, Rulfo) · Borges, Neruda, Paz, Mistral · Post-Boom (Allende, Bolaño) · Testimonio (Menchú)

~33%
🏛️

Domain C — Culture & Civilization

History of Spain (Reconquista → Inquisition → Civil War → Franco → 1978 Constitution) · Pre-Columbian civilizations (Aztec, Maya, Inca) · Colonial Latin America and independence · Art (Velázquez, Goya, Picasso, Rivera, Kahlo, Gaudí) · Music (flamenco, tango, cumbia, mariachi) · Cultural traditions (Día de los Muertos, mestizaje) · Geography and regional identities

~33%

Most commonly underestimated: Domain C. Most candidates prepare well for literature but are surprised by how many questions test art history, pre-Columbian civilizations, and music origins. Budget dedicated study time for all three domains — not just the literary ones.

Schedule Your Exam

When your practice test scores consistently clear 78% across all three domains, you are ready. See the test dates guide for registration steps, score release timeline, and exam day checklist.

Subtest I vs. Subtest II vs. Subtest III

Three subtests — three very different types of knowledge. Here is how they compare.

Order matters: The subtests can be taken in any order with no prerequisites. However, most candidates benefit from separating them — Subtest I and II test entirely different knowledge bases, and cramming both simultaneously tends to produce weaker scores on each. Give each subtest its own dedicated preparation window.
