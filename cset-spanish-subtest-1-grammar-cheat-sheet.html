Subjunctive Mood — WEIRDO Triggers

The subjunctive is required after expressions falling into one of six categories, remembered by the acronym WEIRDO.

W
Wishes
querer que, esperar que, desear que
E
Emotion
alegrarse de que, temer que
I
Impersonal expressions
es importante que, es posible que
R
Requests
pedir que, sugerir que, recomendar que
D
Doubt/Denial
dudar que, no creer que, negar que
O
Ojalá
ojalá que + subjunctive
ContextRuleExample
Adjective clausesSubjunctive when antecedent is unknown/non-existentBusco un libro que tenga esa información.
Temporal conjunctionsSubjunctive when referring to future/hypothetical timeLlámame cuando llegues.
Negated belief verbsSubjunctive after no creer que, no pensar queNo creo que él tenga razón.

Ser vs. Estar

Use SER forUse ESTAR for
Identity / profession — Es maestra.Location — Está en la escuela.
Origin / nationality — Es de México.Temporary condition — Está cansado.
Inherent characteristics — Es alto.Ongoing action (progressive) — Está hablando.
Time / dates — Son las tres.Resulting state (past participle) — La puerta está abierta.
Material / possession — Es de madera.Emotional/physical state — Está feliz.

Exam trap

Ser + past participle = passive voice (action by an agent). Estar + past participle = resulting condition. La puerta fue abierta por el viento (was opened, action) vs. La puerta está abierta (is open, state).

Preterite vs. Imperfect

Use Preterite forUse Imperfect for
Completed actions with clear endpoint — Comí a las ocho.Habitual/repeated past actions — Comía a las ocho todos los días.
Sequence of events — Llegó, abrió la puerta y entró.Background description / ongoing state — Hacía frío y llovía.
Interrupting action — Sonó el teléfono.Action in progress when interrupted — Dormía cuando sonó el teléfono.

Por vs. Para

Use POR forUse PARA for
Duration — por tres horasRecipient — para mi madre
Movement through — caminar por el parquePurpose / goal — estudiar para el examen
Cause/reason — por la lluviaDeadline — para el lunes
Exchange — gracias por tu ayudaDestination — salir para España

Object Pronoun Order

When indirect and direct object pronouns appear together, indirect comes before direct, and both precede a conjugated verb.

EnglishSpanishOrder
She explains it to us.Ella nos lo explica.nos (indirect) + lo (direct)
I gave it to him.Yo se lo di.le→se (before lo/la) + lo

Special rule: Indirect object pronouns le/les become se when followed by a direct object pronoun starting with l- (lo, la, los, las).

Si Clauses (Hypotheticals)

TypeStructureExample
Real/likely conditionsi + present, present/future/commandSi estudias, aprobarás.
Contrary-to-fact (present)si + imperfect subjunctive, conditionalSi tuviera tiempo, viajaría.
Contrary-to-fact (past)si + pluperfect subjunctive, conditional perfectSi hubiera estudiado, habría aprobado.

Never use the conditional tense directly after si in a hypothetical clause — this is one of the most common errors tested on the exam.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the WEIRDO acronym used for in Spanish grammar?
WEIRDO is a memory device for the categories of expressions that trigger the subjunctive mood: Wishes, Emotion, Impersonal expressions, Requests, Doubt/Denial, and Ojalá. If a sentence contains a trigger from one of these categories, the verb in the dependent clause typically takes the subjunctive.
What is the easiest way to remember when to use ser versus estar?
A common shortcut is that ser describes inherent, permanent characteristics (identity, origin, profession, time), while estar describes temporary states, conditions, locations, and ongoing actions. Exceptions exist, such as estar with past participles for resulting states, so context still matters.
How is preterite different from imperfect?
The preterite describes a completed action with a clear beginning and end, while the imperfect describes ongoing, habitual, or background actions in the past without a defined endpoint. They are often combined in one sentence, with the imperfect setting the scene and the preterite describing an interrupting event.
Is this grammar cheat sheet enough to pass CSET Spanish Subtest I on its own?
No. This cheat sheet is a quick-reference tool for the most heavily tested grammar points, not a complete preparation resource.
