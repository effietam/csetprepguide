Krashen's Five Hypotheses — Quick Reference

  • Acquisition-Learning Hypothesis — subconscious acquisition vs. conscious learning are distinct processes
  • Natural Order Hypothesis — grammatical structures are acquired in a predictable order
  • Monitor Hypothesis — learned knowledge acts as an editor, not a generator, of language
  • Input Hypothesis (i+1) — acquisition occurs with comprehensible input slightly above current level
  • Affective Filter Hypothesis — emotional state can block or allow input to be acquired

For constructed-response writing

Using 3–4 different cohesive devices in a single written response demonstrates vocabulary range and organizational skill — both scored criteria. Avoid repeating pero (informal "but") in formal writing; use sin embargo or no obstante instead.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to memorize all of these vocabulary words?
No. Focus first on the false cognates and academic/SLA terminology sections, since these appear most consistently on the exam. The cohesive devices and idioms sections build reading and writing fluency but are lower priority than the core grammar and linguistics vocabulary.
Are false cognates really tested on CSET Spanish Subtest I?
Yes. False cognates appear regularly in both multiple-choice vocabulary questions and as traps in reading comprehension passages. Words like embarazado, sensible, and éxito are commonly tested because they are easy to misinterpret if you rely on English intuition.
What is the difference between seseo and yeísmo?
Seseo is the pronunciation of c (before e/i) and z as /s/, standard across most of Latin America. Yeísmo is the merging of ll and y into a single sound, dominant in most of the Spanish-speaking world. Both are regional features tested in the Domain 1 sociolinguistics content.
How should I use this vocabulary list to study?
Work through one section per study session rather than trying to memorize everything at once. Write example sentences using each new word, then test yourself by covering the definitions and recalling them from the Spanish term alone. Revisit the false cognates and SLA terminology sections most frequently, since these are the highest-yield content.
Is this vocabulary list enough to pass on its own?
No single resource is sufficient alone. Use this vocabulary list alongside the full Study Guide and Practice Test to cover grammar, reading comprehension, and oral communication theory in addition to vocabulary.
