What is CSET Spanish Subtest I?

CSET Spanish Subtest I (Test Code 145) is the first of three subtests required for a California Single Subject Teaching Credential in World Language: Spanish. It is administered by Pearson on behalf of the California Commission on Teacher Credentialing (CTC).

Subtest I focuses on oral and written communication — grammar, reading comprehension, vocabulary, phonology, and second language acquisition theory. It is taken separately from Subtest II (146) and Subtest III (147).

Who needs to take CSET Spanish Subtest I?

Anyone pursuing a California Single Subject Teaching Credential in Spanish who has not completed an approved subject matter preparation program. This includes most candidates who want to teach Spanish in California public schools at the secondary level (grades 7–12).

If you completed a CTC-approved subject matter program at a California university, you may be exempt. Check with your credential program advisor to confirm.

How difficult is CSET Spanish Subtest I?

It depends on your background. The exam tests academic and professional-level Spanish, not just conversational fluency. The biggest surprise for most candidates is Domain 1 — which covers linguistics theory, phonology, and second language acquisition concepts that go well beyond everyday Spanish use.

Candidates who prepare consistently for 6–10 weeks typically pass on their first attempt. See the full difficulty breakdown in the How Hard Is CSET Spanish Subtest I? guide.

How many questions are on CSET Spanish Subtest I?

Approximately 50 multiple-choice questions and 2–3 constructed-response questions. The total testing time is approximately 3 hours. Multiple-choice questions have four answer options (A–D) with no penalty for wrong answers. Constructed-response questions require written answers in formal Spanish.

What score do I need to pass?

The passing score is 220 on a scaled score range of 100 to 300. This is not a percentage — a scaled score accounts for small differences in difficulty across exam versions. A score of exactly 220 is a passing score.

Each of the three Spanish subtests must be passed independently with 220 or higher. See the full explanation in the Passing Score guide.

How much does CSET Spanish Subtest I cost?

The registration fee is approximately $99 per subtest as of 2026. If you take all three subtests, the total cost is approximately $297. Always confirm the current fee at ctcexams.nesinc.com before registering, as fees can change.

Is there a fee waiver for the CSET?

Pearson does not offer a general public fee waiver for the CSET. However, some credential programs and school districts offer financial assistance or reimbursement for exam fees. Check with your teacher preparation program or employing district for any available support. Some California programs offer incentives for candidates in high-need subject areas including World Languages.

How long should I study for CSET Spanish Subtest I?

It depends on your Spanish background:

  • Native or heritage speaker: 4–6 weeks focused on linguistics theory and formal writing
  • Spanish major or minor: 4–6 weeks reviewing exam-specific topics
  • Fluent non-native speaker: 6–8 weeks
  • Advanced learner: 10–12 weeks

For a day-by-day plan, see the 30-Day CSET Spanish Study Plan.

Can I retake CSET Spanish Subtest I if I do not pass?

Yes. There is no limit on the number of attempts and no mandatory waiting period between attempts. You can register for a retake as soon as you receive your scores. The full registration fee applies for each attempt. Use your score report's domain feedback to target your weakest areas before retaking.

Do CSET scores expire?

No. Once you pass a subtest, that result is permanent. You never need to retake a subtest you have already passed, regardless of how much time passes between subtests.

When will I receive my score report?

Score reports are typically available within approximately 5 business weeks after your test date. You will receive an email notification when your scores are posted in your Pearson Evaluation Systems account. Scores are not available at the testing center when you finish.

Scores are automatically reported to the California CTC — you do not need to submit them separately.

Do I have to take all three CSET Spanish subtests at once?

No. You can take the subtests in any order and on different days. Many candidates take one subtest at a time and prepare for each separately. Taking them one at a time allows you to focus your preparation and avoid burnout.

Can I use a Spanish dictionary during the exam?

No. No reference materials of any kind are permitted. The exam is completely closed-book and closed-notes. You will be given scratch paper at the testing center for notes during the exam.

Is CSET Spanish Subtest I easier if I am a native speaker?

Not necessarily easier overall — differently challenging. Native and heritage speakers often have strong oral and reading skills but may have gaps in formal grammar rules and the linguistics theory tested in Domain 1 (second language acquisition, phonology, morphology). These are learnable with focused preparation. Many native speakers are surprised by the academic linguistics content.

Can I take CSET Spanish Subtest I online from home?

Online proctored options may be available. Availability changes over time. Check current options at ctcexams.nesinc.com when you register. In-person testing at a Pearson Vue testing center is always available.
