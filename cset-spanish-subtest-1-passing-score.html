What Is the Passing Score for CSET Spanish Subtest 1?

220
Passing Score — CSET Spanish Subtest 1 (145)
On a scaled score range of 100 – 300

To pass CSET Spanish Subtest 1 (Test Code 145), you need a scaled score of 220 or higher on a scale of 100 to 300. A score of exactly 220 is a passing score.

This threshold applies to each subtest independently. Your score on Subtest 1 does not combine with or offset your scores on Subtest 2 (146) or Subtest 3 (147) — each must be passed separately.

How Scaled Scoring Works

The 100–300 scale is not a percentage. It does not mean you need to answer 220 out of 300 questions correctly. CSET uses scaled scoring to account for small differences in difficulty across different exam versions.

  • 300 — the highest possible score
  • 220 — the passing threshold (demonstrates required competency)
  • 100 — the lowest possible reported score

Because scores are scaled, you cannot calculate your result by counting right and wrong answers. A raw score of, say, 38 out of 50 correct on the multiple-choice section gets converted into a scaled score through a statistical process. The conversion varies slightly by exam version.

What percentage correct do you need?

Pearson does not publish an official raw-score-to-scaled-score conversion table. Based on the structure of the exam — approximately 50 multiple-choice questions plus 2–3 constructed-response questions — most candidates who pass report answering roughly 70–75% of multiple-choice questions correctly and performing at a mid-range level on the constructed-response rubric. These are estimates, not guarantees. Focus on understanding the material deeply rather than targeting a specific percentage.

How to use domain feedback if you did not pass

The domain performance profile tells you whether your relative weakness was in Domain 1 (Oral Communication — phonology, SLA theory, oral discourse) or Domain 2 (Written Communication — grammar, reading comprehension, vocabulary). Use this directly to prioritize your study plan before a retake. Domain 1 gap: focus on Krashen's hypotheses, Spanish phonology, and regional variation. Domain 2 gap: focus on subjunctive, ser/estar, preterite/imperfect, and reading comprehension strategies.

When Will You Get Your Results?

Score reports are typically available within approximately five business weeks after your test date. You will receive an email notification when your scores are posted in your Pearson account.

Scores are not available at the testing center when you finish. Log in to your account at ctcexams.nesinc.com to check when your report is ready.

Planning tip

If you need your scores before a credential program deadline, schedule your exam at least 6–7 weeks before that date to allow for the full reporting window.

Retake Policy

  • There is no mandatory waiting period — you can register for a retake immediately after receiving your scores
  • There is no limit on the number of attempts
  • You pay the full registration fee (approximately $99 as of 2026) for each attempt
  • Scores do not expire — once you pass a subtest, you never need to retake it
  • Subtests are independent — passing Subtest 2 or 3 previously is permanent regardless of Subtest 1 outcomes

How to prepare for a retake

Use your score report's domain performance profile as your study roadmap. A targeted 4–6 week focused review of your weaker domain — combined with additional constructed-response writing practice — is more effective than re-studying everything from scratch.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is 220 out of 300 the same as 73%?
No. The 100–300 scale is not a percentage. You cannot calculate your likelihood of passing by dividing 220 by 300. The scaled score reflects your performance relative to the difficulty of the specific questions on your exam, not a raw count of correct answers.
Can I combine scores across subtests to pass?
No. Each subtest must be passed independently with a score of 220 or higher. A very high score on Subtest 2 does not offset a low score on Subtest 1.
Do CSET scores expire?
No. Once you pass a subtest, that result is permanent. You do not need to retake any subtest you have already passed, regardless of how much time passes.
Will I find out my score at the testing center?
No. Scores are not available immediately after the exam. Results are posted to your Pearson account approximately five business weeks after your test date.
How do I send my scores to the CTC?
Scores are automatically reported to the California CTC. You do not need to submit them separately when applying for your credential.
