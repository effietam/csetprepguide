What Is the CSET Spanish Subtest 1?

The CSET Spanish Subtest 1 (Test Code 145) is one of three required subtests that California teacher credential candidates must pass to demonstrate subject matter competency in Spanish. It is administered by Pearson on behalf of the California Commission on Teacher Credentialing (CTC).

CSET stands for California Subject Examinations for Teachers. These exams replace traditional subject matter preparation programs — instead of completing a content-focused undergraduate major, you can demonstrate your knowledge by passing the CSET.

Subtest 1 specifically focuses on your ability to use Spanish effectively in oral and written communication — grammar, reading comprehension, vocabulary, oral language features, and foundational linguistics concepts relevant to language teachers.

Quick Facts: CSET Spanish Subtest 1

  • Test Code: 145
  • Full exam name: CSET: World Languages — Spanish, Subtest I
  • Administered by: Pearson Evaluation Systems
  • Credential required for: Single Subject Teaching Credential in Spanish
  • Format: Multiple-choice + constructed-response questions
  • Passing score: 220 (on a scale of 100–300)
  • Register at: ctcexams.nesinc.com

Who Needs to Take It?

You need to pass all three CSET Spanish subtests if you are pursuing a California Single Subject Teaching Credential in World Language: Spanish. This credential is required to teach Spanish at the secondary level (grades 7–12) in California public schools.

You may also need it if you are pursuing a bilingual authorization or any credential that includes Spanish as a subject matter area.

Note: If you completed an approved subject matter preparation program at a California university, you may be exempt from the CSET. Check with your credential program advisor to confirm whether you need to take the exam.

You do not need to be a native Spanish speaker to pass — but you do need genuine proficiency. The exam tests academic and professional-level Spanish, not conversational fluency alone.

The Three CSET Spanish Subtests

CSET Spanish consists of three separate subtests. You must pass all three, but you can take them in any order and on different days.

What Subtest 1 Covers: The Two Domains

The official CSET Spanish Subject Matter Requirements (SMRs) divide Subtest 1 into two main domains:

Domain 1: Oral Communication

This domain tests your knowledge of how Spanish works in spoken contexts:

  • Phonology — the Spanish sound system, vowels, consonants (including the r/rr distinction), and stress patterns
  • Regional variation — features like seseo, yeísmo, and dialectal differences across Spanish-speaking regions
  • Oral discourse — how spoken Spanish differs from written Spanish, discourse markers, and intonation
  • Listening comprehension — strategies for understanding and processing spoken Spanish
  • Second language acquisition theory — Krashen's hypotheses (comprehensible input, affective filter, monitor), code-switching, interlanguage, critical period hypothesis

Domain 2: Written Communication

This is typically the larger portion of the exam. It covers:

  • Grammar and usage — subjunctive mood, ser vs. estar, por/para, object pronouns, tenses (preterite, imperfect, pluperfect, conditional), commands, and more
  • Reading comprehension — main idea, supporting details, inference, author's purpose, vocabulary in context, text structure
  • Vocabulary — false cognates, idiomatic expressions, register, word families, cohesive devices
  • Writing conventions — standard Spanish orthography, punctuation, sentence structure

Most heavily tested grammar topics

  • Subjunctive mood — triggers, uses, and common errors
  • Ser vs. estar — permanent vs. temporary distinctions
  • Preterite vs. imperfect — aspect and context
  • Por vs. para — purpose, duration, movement, recipient
  • Object pronouns — placement and order
  • Commands — affirmative and negative tú and usted forms
  • Si clauses — hypothetical and contrary-to-fact conditions

How Difficult Is the CSET Spanish Subtest 1?

The exam is designed to test professional-level Spanish knowledge, not conversational ability. Candidates who struggle most are typically those who:

  • Are heritage Spanish speakers with strong oral skills but gaps in formal grammar knowledge
  • Have not studied Spanish formally for several years
  • Are unfamiliar with the oral communication and linguistics content in Domain 1

Candidates who prepare thoroughly and have solid academic Spanish typically pass on the first attempt. Pearson does not publish official pass rates for individual subtests.

How to Prepare

1

Download the official Subject Matter Requirements

The SMRs are the official blueprint for every question. Download them free at ctcexams.nesinc.com and use them as your study checklist.

2

Take a diagnostic practice test first

Before studying, find your gaps. Take a full set of practice questions, identify your weak domains, and build your study plan from there.

3

Study grammar rules explicitly

Even native speakers benefit from reviewing formal grammar rules. Focus on subjunctive, ser/estar, and preterite/imperfect — these appear consistently on every practice test.

4

Study the SLA theory for Domain 1

Many candidates overlook this content. Make sure you understand Krashen's Input Hypothesis (i+1), Affective Filter, Monitor Hypothesis, Natural Order, code-switching, interlanguage, and fossilization.

5

Practice constructed-response writing

The real exam includes written responses in Spanish. Practice writing short formal paragraphs — your grammar needs to hold up in production, not just in multiple-choice recognition.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I take Subtest 1 without taking Subtests 2 and 3 on the same day?
Yes. You can take each subtest separately on different days and in any order. Many candidates prepare for one subtest at a time and schedule them several weeks apart.
Is the exam harder if I'm a heritage Spanish speaker?
Not necessarily harder overall, but differently challenging. Heritage speakers often have strong oral and reading skills but may have gaps in formal grammar rules and the linguistics theory content (Domain 1). These are learnable with focused preparation.
Do I need to study Spanish literature for Subtest 1?
No. Literary analysis and major authors are covered in Subtest 2 (Test Code 146). Subtest 1 focuses on grammar, reading comprehension, vocabulary, oral communication, and linguistics only.
How long should I spend preparing?
It depends on your current level. Candidates with strong academic Spanish backgrounds typically feel ready in 4–6 weeks of focused review. Those with more gaps may need 3–4 months. Take a diagnostic practice test first to calibrate.
Where can I find the official preparation materials?
The official Subject Matter Requirements, sample questions, and test structure overview are available free at ctcexams.nesinc.com. Search for CSET Spanish (145/146/147).
