Exam Format: Question Types and Time

CSET Spanish Subtest 1 (Test Code 145) consists of two types of questions:

Question TypeNumberWhat It Tests
Multiple Choice ~50 questions Grammar, reading comprehension, vocabulary, oral communication concepts — one correct answer from four options (A, B, C, D)
Constructed Response 2–3 questions Written responses in Spanish — you write several sentences or a short paragraph demonstrating written communication proficiency

The total time allowed is approximately 3 hours per subtest. Most candidates have sufficient time — pacing is rarely reported as a problem on Subtest 1.

The exam is administered computer-based at Pearson Vue testing centers. You type your constructed-response answers directly into the testing interface.

How Multiple-Choice Questions Work

Each multiple-choice question has four answer options (A, B, C, D). There is no penalty for wrong answers, so answer every question — never leave one blank. When unsure, eliminate clearly wrong options first and choose from what remains.

How Constructed-Response Questions Work

Constructed-response questions ask you to write directly in Spanish. A typical prompt might ask you to write a short formal paragraph on an educational topic, correct a passage with grammatical errors and explain the corrections, or respond to questions about a Spanish text.

Your responses are scored by trained raters on a rubric evaluating accuracy, vocabulary range, grammar, and organization. Write in complete sentences and use formal register — avoid slang.

Passing Score Explained

The passing score for CSET Spanish Subtest 1 is 220 out of a possible 300.

CSET scores are reported on a scaled score range of 100–300. This is not a percentage. It reflects your performance relative to the difficulty of the specific questions on your exam version.

Scaled ScoreResult
220–300Pass — meets the competency standard
100–219Did not pass — score report shows which domains need improvement
220 exactlyPass — 220 is a passing score

If you do not pass, your score report will include a diagnostic feedback profile showing your relative performance in Domain 1 (Oral Communication) and Domain 2 (Written Communication). Use this to focus your preparation for a retake.

Important: Each of the three CSET Spanish subtests (145, 146, 147) must be passed separately with a score of 220 or higher. Passing one subtest does not apply toward another.

How to Register: Step by Step

1

Go to the official registration website

Visit ctcexams.nesinc.com — the official California Educator Credentialing Examinations site. Do not register through any other site.

2

Create or log in to your account

You need a Pearson Evaluation Systems account. Use your legal name exactly as it appears on your ID — it must match at check-in.

3

Select the CSET Spanish exam

Navigate to the CSET section, search for "CSET: World Languages — Spanish," and select Subtest I (Test Code 145).

4

Choose your test date and location

Select a Pearson Vue testing center and date that gives you enough preparation time. Most candidates schedule 6–10 weeks out. Check for online proctored options as well.

5

Pay the registration fee

Each subtest has a registration fee — approximately $99 per subtest as of 2026. Confirm the exact current fee on the registration site before paying.

6

Save your admission ticket

After payment, download or print your admission ticket. You will need it on test day. Save the email confirmation as a backup.

Test Dates and Scheduling

The CSET does not have fixed national test dates. It is offered on a continuous basis at Pearson Vue centers, so you can typically schedule within a few weeks of deciding to register.

Availability depends on your location. Popular California testing centers typically have slots within 1–3 weeks. If you are in a less populated area, plan further ahead.

Scheduling tip

Book your exam date before you feel fully ready — a fixed date creates accountability and prevents indefinite postponement. Most candidates study effectively in 6–10 weeks. Set the date and work backward to build your schedule.

Test Day: What to Bring

Bring these

  • Your admission ticket — printed or on your phone
  • Two valid forms of ID — primary ID must be government-issued with photo and signature (driver's license or passport). Name must match your registration exactly.
  • Arrive 30 minutes early — late arrivals may be turned away

Do not bring

  • Notes, books, or study materials of any kind
  • Electronic devices (phones, smartwatches)
  • Food or drink

You will be photographed and asked to store personal belongings in a locker. Scratch paper is provided for notes during the exam. You can flag questions to return to before submitting.

Results: Timeline and What They Mean

Score reports are typically available within approximately five business weeks after your test date. You will receive an email notification when scores are posted in your Pearson account.

Your score report shows your scaled score (100–300), whether you passed or did not pass, and — if you did not pass — a domain performance profile showing relative strength in each area.

Scores are automatically reported to the California CTC. You do not need to submit them separately when applying for your credential.

Retake Policy

  • There is no mandatory waiting period between attempts — you can register for a retake immediately after receiving your scores
  • There is no limit on the number of attempts
  • You pay the full registration fee for each attempt
  • Use your score report's diagnostic profile to focus your preparation before retaking

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I use a Spanish dictionary during the exam?
No. No reference materials of any kind are permitted. The test is closed-book and closed-notes.
Can I take CSET Spanish Subtest 1 online from home?
Online proctored options may be available. Check the current options at ctcexams.nesinc.com when you register — availability changes over time.
What if I need testing accommodations (extra time, large print, etc.)?
Testing accommodations are available for candidates with documented disabilities. Apply through Pearson before registering for your exam date. The process requires documentation from a qualified professional — do not leave this to the last minute.
Does a passing CSET score expire?
No. CSET scores do not expire. Once you pass a subtest, you do not need to retake it.
Is CSET Spanish the same as CSET Multiple Subjects?
No. CSET Multiple Subjects (Subtests I, II, III) is for candidates pursuing an elementary school credential. CSET Spanish (145, 146, 147) is for candidates pursuing a Single Subject credential in World Language: Spanish. They are entirely different exams.
