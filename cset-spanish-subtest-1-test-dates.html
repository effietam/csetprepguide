How Scheduling Works

Unlike many standardized tests, the CSET does not have set national test dates published in advance. Instead, it is offered on a continuous, year-round basis at Pearson Vue testing centers across California and nationwide.

This means you pick your own date based on your preparation timeline and seat availability at your preferred testing center. Most popular California centers (Los Angeles, San Diego, San Francisco Bay Area, Sacramento) typically have available seats within 1–3 weeks of when you search.

You can also check for online proctored options during registration — availability varies and is subject to change.

Computer-Based Testing

CSET Spanish Subtest I is administered entirely on a computer at a Pearson Vue testing center. You type your constructed-response answers directly into the testing interface. Scratch paper is provided. No personal devices are permitted in the testing room.

How to Register Step by Step

1

Go to the official site

Visit ctcexams.nesinc.com — the only official registration site for California educator credentialing exams.

2

Create or log into your account

Use your legal name exactly as it appears on your government-issued ID. The name must match at check-in or you may be turned away.

3

Select CSET Spanish Subtest I

Find "CSET: World Languages — Spanish" and select Subtest I (Test Code 145).

4

Choose your date and location

Search available testing centers near you and select a date that gives you enough preparation time. Online proctored options may also appear here.

5

Pay the registration fee

Approximately $99 per subtest as of 2026. Confirm the current fee before paying.

6

Save your admission ticket

Download or print your admission ticket after payment. You will need it on test day along with two valid forms of ID.

Planning Your Timeline

Because you set your own date, the key is working backward from your deadline — whether that is a credential program deadline, a school district start date, or a personal goal.

10–12 weeks out
Register for your exam date. Lock in the date before you feel fully ready — having a deadline prevents indefinite postponement.
Weeks 1–4
Study Domain 1: phonology, morphology, syntax, SLA theory, sociolinguistics. These take the most time for most candidates.
Weeks 5–8
Study Domain 2: grammar, reading comprehension, vocabulary. Begin daily constructed-response writing practice.
Weeks 9–10
Full timed practice tests. Review every missed question. Focus remaining time on your weakest domain.
1 day before
Light review only. Prepare your admission ticket and ID. Get a full night of sleep.
Test day
Arrive 30 minutes early. Bring admission ticket + two valid IDs. Primary ID must be government-issued with photo and signature.
5 weeks after
Score report posted to your Pearson account. Email notification sent when ready.

Scheduling tip

If you have a credential program deadline or district hiring date, schedule your exam at least 7–8 weeks before that date. This gives you 5 weeks for score reporting plus a buffer if you need to retake.

Score Release Schedule

Score reports are typically available within approximately 5 business weeks after your test date. Pearson sends an email notification when your report is ready in your account.

Your score report includes your scaled score (100–300), a pass or did-not-pass result, and — if you did not pass — a domain performance profile showing your relative strength in each area.

Scores are automatically reported to the California CTC. You do not need to submit them separately.

Planning note: Do not schedule your exam less than 6 weeks before a hard credential program deadline. The 5-week score reporting window leaves very little room for delays.

Rescheduling and Cancellation

Pearson allows rescheduling and cancellation, but policies and fees apply depending on how far in advance you make changes. Always check the current policy at ctcexams.nesinc.com when you register. Generally:

  • Rescheduling well in advance (several weeks) is usually free or low-cost
  • Last-minute cancellations (within 24–48 hours) may result in forfeiting your fee
  • No-shows typically forfeit the full registration fee

Frequently Asked Questions

Are there specific CSET test dates I need to register for?
No. The CSET is offered year-round on a continuous basis. You choose your own date based on seat availability at your preferred testing center. There are no nationally published test date windows.
How far in advance should I register?
Most candidates register 6–10 weeks before their target exam date. Registering early locks in your preferred date and gives you a clear study deadline. Popular testing centers fill up faster — register earlier if you are in a high-demand area.
Can I take the exam more than once in the same month?
There is no minimum waiting period between attempts. You can theoretically register for a retake immediately after receiving your scores. However, the 5-week score reporting window means you would be waiting at least 5 weeks before you know your result from the first attempt.
What happens if I miss my exam appointment?
If you do not show up and did not cancel in advance per Pearson's cancellation policy, you typically forfeit your registration fee. You would need to register and pay again for a new appointment.
