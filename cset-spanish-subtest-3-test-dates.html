CSET Spanish Subtest III Test Dates & Score Release Schedule

🗓️ Test Code 147 📍 Year-round availability ⏱ Scores in 3–5 weeks 📅 Updated 2026

CSET Spanish Subtest III (Test Code 147) is available year-round at Pearson VUE test centers and via online proctored testing. Because constructed-response questions require human scoring, there are no instant results — plan for a 3–5 week wait. This page covers everything you need to schedule strategically: when to register, when to expect scores, and how to work backward from your credential program deadline.

⚡ Key Scheduling Facts

  • Testing is available year-round — no fixed national test dates. You choose when to go.
  • Score reports are released approximately 3–5 weeks after your test date on scheduled release dates.
  • Register at least 2–3 weeks in advance; 4–6 weeks ahead for high-demand periods (May–June, November–December).
  • Retake wait period: 45 calendar days minimum between attempts.
  • Online proctored testing is available on most dates — no need to travel to a test center.
  • Always verify current dates and availability at ctcexams.nesinc.com — this is the only official source.

Year-Round Availability

Unlike many standardized exams that offer only a few fixed national test dates per year, CSET Spanish Subtest III is available on a continuous, year-round basis at Pearson VUE test centers across California and the United States. You select your own date and location when you register — there are no mandatory national test windows.

Testing is conducted Monday through Saturday at most Pearson VUE test centers, with some centers offering Sunday appointments. Online proctored testing through Pearson OnVUE provides even greater scheduling flexibility, including early morning and evening slots that test centers may not offer.

Important: Specific date availability depends entirely on your chosen test center or online proctoring capacity — not on a published calendar. Seats fill on a rolling first-come, first-served basis. Always check real-time availability at ctcexams.nesinc.com. The date tables on this page are planning guides only — verify every date through Pearson before making any commitments.

Score Release Schedule

Because CSET Spanish Subtest III includes two constructed-response questions scored by trained human raters, scores cannot be released immediately after the exam. Pearson publishes a score release calendar, and your results are posted on the next scheduled release date following your exam — typically 3–5 weeks after you test.

How the Score Release Process Works

📝
Day 0

You Complete the Exam

Multiple-choice responses are captured electronically. Constructed-response essays are transmitted to trained raters.

👥
Days 1–14

Constructed Responses Are Scored

Two independent raters score each constructed response on a 1–4 scale. If raters disagree by more than one point, a third rater resolves the discrepancy.

📊
Days 14–25

Scores Are Compiled and Scaled

Multiple-choice and constructed-response scores are combined and converted to the 100–300 scaled score. Quality checks are completed.

📧
Days 21–35

Score Report Released

You receive an email notification. Your score report — including scaled score, pass/fail status, domain performance indicators, and constructed-response scores — becomes available in your ctcexams.nesinc.com account.

🎓
After Release

Results Transmitted to CTC

Passing scores are automatically transmitted to the California Commission on Teacher Credentialing. No separate action is required on your part.

Plan accordingly: If you have a credential program deadline, application deadline, or student teaching start date that depends on your CSET scores, count back at least 5–6 weeks from that deadline to identify your latest acceptable test date. Earlier is always safer.

High-Demand Periods to Know

Two windows each year generate significantly higher demand for CSET test center seats in California:

Spring Rush: April–June

California credential programs typically have spring application deadlines, and many candidates completing their preparation in the spring concentrate test appointments in April through June. Test centers in the Los Angeles, San Diego, Inland Empire, San Francisco Bay Area, and Sacramento regions fill fastest during this window. If your credential program deadline falls in summer or early fall, test in March to avoid the spring rush entirely.

Fall Rush: October–November

A secondary demand peak occurs in October and November as candidates prepare for spring credential program cohorts with December or January application deadlines. Center availability tightens through mid-November, and December capacity is further reduced by holiday closures at many Pearson VUE locations.

Online proctored testing (Pearson OnVUE) provides a useful alternative during high-demand periods — it is not subject to test center seat limits. If your preferred test center is fully booked, check whether the online proctored option has availability on your desired date.

How to Register Step by Step

1

Go to ctcexams.nesinc.com

This is the only official registration portal for CSET exams. Create an account if you do not already have one. Your account name must exactly match your government-issued photo ID.

2

Select CSET Spanish Subtest III (Test Code 147)

Search for "CSET Spanish" or enter Test Code 147 directly. Confirm you are registering for Subtest III specifically — the three Spanish subtests are listed separately.

3

Choose Your Testing Format

Select either a Pearson VUE test center appointment or online proctored testing (Pearson OnVUE). For online testing, ensure your computer meets system requirements before selecting this option.

4

Select a Date, Time, and Location

Browse available dates at your preferred test center or select an online proctored time slot. For test centers, enter your zip code to find nearby locations. Book your preferred date — availability is real-time and seats are not held.

5

Pay the Registration Fee

Complete payment by credit or debit card. Fee waivers may be available for qualifying candidates — check the CTC website before paying. Save your confirmation number and appointment details.

6

Prepare and Show Up

Bring a valid government-issued photo ID with a name matching your registration exactly. Arrive 15 minutes early for test center appointments. For online testing, complete the system check and check-in process at your scheduled time.

Frequently Asked Questions

When can I take CSET Spanish Subtest III? ▾
CSET Spanish Subtest III is offered year-round at Pearson VUE test centers throughout California and nationally, and via online proctored testing on most dates. Specific available dates and seats are shown in real time when you register at ctcexams.nesinc.com. Register at least two to three weeks in advance to secure your preferred date and location.
How long after the test will I receive my scores? ▾
Score reports for CSET Spanish Subtest III are typically released approximately 3 to 5 weeks after your test date, on the next scheduled score release date. Because Subtest III includes constructed-response questions scored by human raters, scores cannot be released immediately. You will receive an email notification when your score report is available in your account.
How far in advance should I register? ▾
Register at least two to three weeks before your desired test date to ensure availability. For high-demand periods — particularly April through June and October through November — register four to six weeks in advance. Popular test center locations in Los Angeles, San Diego, the Bay Area, and Sacramento fill earliest during peak periods.
Can I reschedule or cancel my appointment? ▾
Yes. You can reschedule or cancel through your ctcexams.nesinc.com account. Rescheduling or canceling at least three business days before your appointment is typically required to avoid forfeiting your full registration fee. Some fees may be retained even with advance notice — review the current cancellation policy on the Pearson website before taking action.
What is the retake waiting period? ▾
Candidates who do not pass must wait at least 45 calendar days before retaking CSET Spanish Subtest III. There is no limit on the total number of retake attempts. The standard registration fee applies for each attempt. The 45-day period begins the day after your test date.
Is online proctored testing available? ▾
Yes. CSET Spanish Subtest III is available as an online proctored exam through Pearson OnVUE. You take the exam on your own computer with a live remote proctor monitoring via webcam. Requirements include a compatible computer, webcam, microphone, stable internet connection, and a private, distraction-free space. Run the system check available on the Pearson website before registering for this format to ensure compatibility.
Are scores automatically sent to CTC after I pass? ▾
Yes. Passing scores are automatically transmitted to the California Commission on Teacher Credentialing (CTC). You do not need to separately send or request score transmission. Your score report in your ctcexams.nesinc.com account serves as your personal record. CTC typically processes score transmissions within a few business days of score release.
