CSET Spanish Subtest II Test Dates: How to Schedule & What to Expect

📅 Year-round scheduling 🗓️ Score release timeline ✅ Exam day checklist 📅 Updated 2026

Unlike many standardized exams, CSET Spanish Subtest II has no fixed test dates — you schedule it year-round at a Pearson VUE test center on a date that fits your preparation timeline. This guide walks you through registration, score release, deadline planning, and exactly what to expect on exam day.

📅 Last updated: June 2026 ⏱️ Reading time: ~8 min 🎯 Test Code 146

  • CSET Subtest II is available year-round — no fixed testing windows, no registration deadlines.

When Is CSET Spanish Subtest II Available?

CSET Spanish Subtest II (Test Code 146) is administered year-round at Pearson VUE test centers. There are no fixed testing windows, no limited registration periods, and no calendar of specific test dates to track. You choose the date and location that fits your schedule.

This is one of the most candidate-friendly aspects of the CSET system. You are never locked into a semester cycle or waiting for the next available window. If you finish your preparation in March, you can test in March. If you need more time and prefer August, schedule August.

Tip: Seat availability varies by test center and time of year. Popular centers in the Bay Area, Los Angeles, and San Diego can fill up weeks in advance, especially in late spring and late summer when many credential candidates are testing. If you have a specific date in mind, register early to lock it in.

How to Register Step by Step

Registration is handled entirely online through the Pearson platform for CSET. The process takes about 10–15 minutes.

1

Go to ctcexams.nesinc.com

This is the official CSET registration portal. Navigate to the CSET section and create an account if you don't already have one, or log in to your existing Pearson account.

2

Select CSET Spanish Subtest II (Test Code 146)

Search for or browse to CSET Spanish Subtest II. Confirm the test code is 146. You can register for multiple subtests in the same session if you plan to take them on the same day — or register separately for each.

3

Choose a test center and date

Enter your zip code or city to find nearby Pearson VUE test centers. Select an available date and time slot. Most centers offer morning and afternoon slots, Monday through Saturday.

4

Pay the registration fee

Registration fees are set by Pearson and subject to change. As of recent administrations, individual subtest fees have been in the $100–$130 range. Check ctcexams.nesinc.com for current pricing. Payment is by credit or debit card.

5

Save your confirmation

You will receive a confirmation email with your appointment details — test center address, date, time, and the Test Code. Save this. You will need your appointment confirmation number if you need to reschedule.

Name match requirement: Your registration name must match the name on your photo ID exactly. If there is a discrepancy, the test center may turn you away. If your legal name has recently changed, update your Pearson account before registering.

What If You Need More Preparation Time?

If 8 weeks before your deadline is not enough preparation time, you have two options: start studying immediately and schedule as early as feasible, or — if possible — request a deadline extension from your credential program. Most programs understand that exam scheduling is outside a candidate's control.

Do not sacrifice preparation quality to hit an early exam date. A not-pass result costs you the registration fee plus 3–4 additional weeks waiting for a retake score. A better-prepared candidate who tests a few weeks later often finishes sooner overall than a rushed candidate who retakes.

Retake planning: If you do not pass and need to retake, factor in another full registration-to-score cycle of approximately 6–8 weeks (preparation + exam + score release). There is no waiting period between attempts, but preparation time is the limiting factor.

What to Expect on Exam Day

Knowing what to expect on exam day reduces stress and helps you arrive focused on performance, not logistics.

Arrival and Check-In

Arrive at the test center 15–30 minutes early. Late arrivals may be turned away and forfeit their registration fee. The check-in process involves presenting your ID, having your palm vein pattern or fingerprint scanned, being photographed, and storing all personal belongings in a locker outside the testing room.

The Testing Environment

You will be seated at a computer workstation in a secure room with other test-takers. The room is quiet and monitored. You will receive scratch paper (or an erasable notepad) from the proctor. You may not bring your own. The testing software walks you through a brief tutorial before the exam begins.

During the Exam

Selected-response questions are answered by clicking your choice. You can flag questions to return to later and review your answers before submitting each section. The constructed-response prompts appear after the selected-response section and are typed directly into the testing software. You have the full allotted time for both sections combined.

After Submitting

After submitting your exam, you may see a preliminary selected-response result on screen — this shows only your multiple-choice performance, not your constructed-response scores. It is not your official result. You will be given a printout of your unofficial result at the test center. Your official score report will be available in your Pearson account within 3–4 weeks.

On the day before: Look up the exact address of your test center, check parking or transit options, and confirm your appointment time. A five-minute drive to an unfamiliar location can take 30 minutes on the wrong day. Knowing exactly where you are going eliminates one source of exam-day stress.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are there specific CSET Spanish Subtest II test dates I need to register by? +

No. CSET Spanish Subtest II is available year-round at Pearson VUE test centers with no fixed registration deadlines or testing windows. You register when you are ready and choose any available date. The only constraint is seat availability at your preferred location.

How far in advance should I register? +

Register at least 2–3 weeks before your target date to ensure seat availability. If you have a credential application deadline, work backward from that deadline and schedule your exam at least 6–8 weeks in advance to allow for the 3–4 week score release period plus CTC processing time.

In high-demand areas (Los Angeles, Bay Area, San Diego), seats at popular centers can fill up quickly, especially from April through September. Registering early gives you the most flexibility in location and time-slot selection.

When are official CSET Spanish Subtest II scores released? +

Official scores are typically released within 3–4 weeks of your testing date. The delay exists because constructed-response questions must be scored by trained human raters before scores can be finalized. You will receive an email notification when your score report is available in your Pearson account at ctcexams.nesinc.com.

A preliminary selected-response result may be visible at the test center immediately after finishing, but this is not your official score and does not include your constructed-response performance.

Can I take Subtest II on the same day as Subtest I? +

Yes. You can register for multiple CSET subtests on the same day and take them back to back in one session at the same test center. Many candidates do this to reduce the number of exam days.

However, Subtest I and Subtest II test very different types of knowledge — active language skills vs. literary and cultural content. Most candidates find that taking them separately with dedicated preparation time for each produces better results than cramming both into one study period.

What ID do I need for the CSET Spanish Subtest II exam? +

You must present two forms of ID at check-in. Your primary ID must be a valid, government-issued photo ID — a driver's license, state ID card, or passport — with your name spelled exactly as it appears in your Pearson registration. A second ID (credit card, student ID, or another government-issued document) is also required.

Name discrepancies between your ID and registration can result in being turned away. If your name has changed, update your Pearson account before exam day.

How many hours should I have between registering and exam day? +

There is no minimum gap between registration and exam day — you could theoretically register and test the next morning if a seat is available. But for most candidates, the practical question is: how much preparation time do I need?

Most candidates need 4–6 weeks of focused preparation. Candidates with a Spanish literature background may need 2–3 weeks. Those with minimal literary background should allow 6–8 weeks. Register your exam date to match your realistic study timeline, not just the earliest available seat.
